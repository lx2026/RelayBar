# RelayBar Privacy Policy

Effective July 15, 2026

RelayBar does not collect, transmit, sell, or share personal data. It contains no analytics, advertising, telemetry, tracking, accounts, or third-party SDKs.

Tunnel names, hosts, ports, imported safe SSH options, and security-scoped bookmarks for user-selected identity files are stored locally in RelayBar's application preferences on the user's Mac. RelayBar sends connection information only to the SSH hosts that the user explicitly configures. SSH authentication is performed by the SSH client supplied by macOS; RelayBar does not request or store passwords or private-key contents.

Removing RelayBar and its application data removes its saved tunnel definitions. Questions or security reports can be submitted through the project's GitHub repository.
